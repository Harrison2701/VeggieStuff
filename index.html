The goal of this app is provide users with an app to assist in cutting the amount of meat eaten

This current version of the app feature a calculator for meat consumption and a drop down menu to find alternatives to your favorite types of meat

  • In the future, we plan to develop a search mechanic to find the best alternatives for your favorite meats

The calculator is designed to show the amount of CO2 emissions and projected water usage needed to produce the meat

  • Select the type of meat that was consumed and estimate the amount of meat that was eaten.
  • Click the "calculate" button to get the estimated results.

According to the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), every American consumes more than 260 lbs carcass weight of meat (red meat and poultry) per year.

With this consumption level per capita, the Americans are the largest group of meat-consumers in the world.

The problem: the consumption of such quantities of meat has a negative impact on the environment and climate.

High meat consumption has an impact on the climate.

The clearing of trees and humus degradation causes large amounts of CO2 to escape into the atmosphere. In addition to that, the animal themselves produce climate-damaging gasses such as methane.

Meat vs Tofu

Both foods contain valuable proteins. Tofu contains 7 to 15% of easily digestible protein components, including all eight essential amino acids. The protein content of meat is 15 to 40%

  • Tofu contains less fat and therefore, your caloric consumption is fewer than when you eat meat.
  • Meat has a significantly higher concentration of B vitamins. Tofu contains large amounts of minerals, above all magnesium and calcium.
  • Tofu is almost cholesterol-free. On the other hand, depending on the type of meat, 1 serving meat (4 oz.) contains 70 to 400 mg of cholesterol.
  • The production of meat is much more resource-intensive and has a much greater impact on the environment and climate than the cultivation of soybeans.
  • Meat has its own characteristic taste. Tofu, on the other hand, tastes rather bland.
